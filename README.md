# CWURP
Carter's Weird Unnamed RTS Project

Hi, I'm Carter, and this is my project. Here's the idea.

Take a framework for an RTS game, and use it to model historical military scenarios as realistically as possible, with a focus on the Pacific theatre of World War II.

This means:
* Real scale, in both space and time
* Ballistic physics simulation for shells and bombs
* Limited fuel and ammunition; units must be acitvely refueled and rearmed
* No shared vision; allied units send sighting reports instead
* Unit sight ranges based on Earth's curvature
* Orders and info must be transmitted between units by signals or radio
* Realistic environmental effects, such as sunlight, wind, and sea state


I'm trying to capture just how difficult the job of a real commander is. You're trying to execute on an operational plan that you have no control over, with tools that are unsuited to the task at hand, with no solid information about enemy forces and scarcely more about your own, and with few opportunities to alter the course of battle once it is joined in earnest.

As of right now, I have no plans for any sort of multiplayer.
